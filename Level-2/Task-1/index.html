Nambi Narayanan

Nambi Narayanan is a renowned Indian scientist and aerospace engineer known for his pioneering work in liquid propulsion systems.

About Nambi Narayanan

Nambi Narayanan was born on December 12, 1941, in Nagercoil, Tamil Nadu, India.

He showed an early aptitude for science and engineering, which led him to pursue a degree in mechanical engineering from Madurai University.

His academic prowess earned him a NASA fellowship, which allowed him to study at the prestigious Princeton University in the United States, where he specialized in chemical rocket propulsion.

Upon returning to India, Narayanan joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1966.

This technology was crucial for ISRO's ambitious plans to develop indigenous satellite launch vehicles.

He quickly became a key figure in India's burgeoning space program, contributing significantly to the development of liquid propulsion technology.

He led the team that developed the Vikas engine, which became a core component of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and the Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV).

In 1994, Narayanan's illustrious career took a dramatic turn when he was falsely accused of espionage.

Narayanan was arrested and subjected to harsh interrogation, which caused significant personal and professional distress.

The Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) took over the case and concluded that the charges against Narayanan were false and fabricated.

In 1996, the Supreme Court of India dismissed all charges against him. Despite his vindication, Narayanan's fight for justice continued as he sought to hold those responsible for his wrongful arrest accountable.

Narayanan's perseverance and dedication to his field were eventually recognized at the highest levels.
